History

I've been interested in technology ever since I was young. The idea that someone could create something so useful with just a few computer parts or lines of code hooked me. Tech remained a hobby as I attended Washington State University, but a Business Administration degree was my main focus.


I quickly realized after graduating that I wanted a more technical career and took a position as an IT technician. I learned as much as I could about how a business runs on tech before eventually starting a new position at Amazon. I assisted in preventing network abuse on the AWS platform, and after a year was promoted to a leadership position for the team I worked in.


Almost immediately after discovering it, I enrolled in General Assembly's Web Development Immersive program. I graduated early 2018 and am now looking for my first full time web development position.

When not programming, you might find me hiking, cooking, traveling (usually with my wonderful girlfriend). I'm definitely more active, less couch potato, but will play the occasional videogame. I'll destress by weightlifting and taking walks through Seattle, typically with an audiobook in my ear. Every now and then I might fall into a craze and build a crypto miner or two.
